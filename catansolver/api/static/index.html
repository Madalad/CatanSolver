Colonist.io 1v1 ruleset

This solver targets the competitive 1-vs-1 mode on Colonist.io, played on the classic base board. It differs from standard 4-player Catan in a few important ways (highlighted below).

Goal

  • First to 15 victory points wins (standard Catan is 10).
  • VP sources: settlement = 1, city = 2, each Victory-Point dev card = 1, Longest Road (5+ road segments) = 2, Largest Army (3+ played knights) = 2.

Board & setup

  • Classic base board: 19 hexes (4 wood, 4 sheep, 4 wheat, 3 brick, 3 ore, 1 desert), 18 number tokens, 9 ports.
  • Number tokens follow the standard spiral layout — balanced so the high-odds 6s and 8s stay spread out (never adjacent) — with a random starting hex and direction.
  • 2 players, snake-draft opening: P1, P2, P2, P1 (each settlement placed with a road). The resources of your second-placed settlement form your opening hand (one card per adjacent producing hex).

Friendly Robber (1v1 difference)

  • When you roll a 7 or play a knight, you move the robber — but you may only steal from / block a player who has at least 3 visible victory points. A player below 3 VP cannot be targeted.

Discarding on a 7 (1v1 difference)

  • You discard half your hand (rounded down) only if you hold more than 9 cards (i.e. 10+).

Trading (1v1 difference)

  • No player-to-player (domestic) trading — in 1v1 a trade mostly helps your only opponent, so it is disabled, matching competitive play.
  • Bank & port (maritime) trades are allowed: 4:1 with the bank, 3:1 at a generic port, 2:1 at a matching-resource port.

Standard components

  • Standard building costs, a bank of 19 cards per resource, and the standard 25-card dev deck (14 knights, 5 victory point, 2 road building, 2 year of plenty, 2 monopoly).

For the full, detailed rules, see the official reference at colonist.io/catan-rules.

How “Analyze” works

You give the solver a board and your draft situation (going first, going second, or your final first-pick). It then ranks every legal opening and highlights the best place(s) to drop your settlement + road.

Each spot gets a strength score — higher is better. It rewards how many resources the spot produces (weighted toward wheat & ore for the 15-point game), how varied those resources are, and whether it touches a useful port. This pre-ranks the candidates fast.

Each candidate also gets a calibrated win % — P(you win) vs an equal-strength bot. We measure how much your opening out-produces your opponent's (with both players completing the rest of the draft at their best opening) and map that gap to a win-% fit on thousands of self-play games. This is the headline number, and we order the picks by it. A balanced opening is ~50/50; the strongest openings reach ~60%, and going first edges going second (best play ≈51% vs ≈45%), in line with the ~56/44 first-mover advantage seen in elite 1v1 play.
